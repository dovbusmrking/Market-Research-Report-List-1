<p><p>The chickpea flour market primarily consists of two types: Desi and Kabuli. Desi chickpeas, smaller and darker, are typically found in South Asian cuisines, offering a robust flavor and higher nutritional content, often used in traditional dishes. Kabuli chickpeas, larger and lighter, are common in Mediterranean and Middle Eastern cuisines, known for their mild taste and smooth texture. Each type caters to distinct culinary preferences and nutritional needs, influencing their market demand and pricing.</p></p>
