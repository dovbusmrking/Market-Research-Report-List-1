<p><p>Potassium sulfate (CAS 7778-80-5) is produced through various processes, primarily including the Mannheim process, which involves reacting potassium chloride with sulfuric acid to create potassium sulfate and hydrochloric acid. Muriate of potash (MOP) is another method, focusing on obtaining potassium chloride for subsequent conversion. The Kieserite process utilizes magnesium sulfate to extract potassium sulfate. Additionally, brine processing leverages natural salt lakes, where potassium is extracted from concentrated salts. Each method has distinct efficiencies and environmental impacts impacting market dynamics.</p></p>
