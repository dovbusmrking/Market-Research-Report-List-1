<p><p>A prosthetic graft is a medical device used to replace or support damaged blood vessels or tissues in surgical procedures. These grafts are made from synthetic or biological materials and are designed to facilitate proper blood flow and healing. Common applications include vascular surgeries, orthopedic procedures, and repair of abdominal aortic aneurysms. The increasing prevalence of cardiovascular diseases, diabetes, and obesity is driving demand for prosthetic grafts, as these conditions often require surgical interventions.</p><p>The prosthetic graft market is experiencing significant growth, projected to expand at a CAGR of 7.5% during the forecast period. This growth is fueled by technological advancements in graft materials and designs, increasing awareness of minimally invasive procedures, and rising healthcare expenditure globally. Additionally, the aging population is contributing to a higher incidence of diseases that necessitate the use of prosthetic grafts. </p><p>Emerging trends include the development of bioengineered grafts, which offer improved biocompatibility and lower rejection rates, as well as innovations in 3D printing technology aimed at creating customized grafts. Overall, the market is poised for continued expansion as healthcare providers seek effective solutions for vascular and tissue repair.</p></p>
