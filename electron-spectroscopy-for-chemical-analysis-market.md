<p><p>Electron Spectroscopy for Chemical Analysis (ESCA) is categorized into two main types: monochromatic and non-monochromatic. Monochromatic systems utilize a single energy level, providing precise energy measurements and enhancing resolution, ideal for analyzing surface chemical states. In contrast, non-monochromatic systems employ a broad energy range, which allows for faster data collection but may introduce overlapping peaks, reducing resolution. Each type serves different analytical needs, balancing detail and efficiency in chemical analysis of materials and surfaces.</p></p>
