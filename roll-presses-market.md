<p><p>The roll presses market comprises hot rolling and cold rolling processes used in metalworking. Hot rolling involves shaping metal at elevated temperatures, enhancing ductility and reducing strength, ideal for large structural components. In contrast, cold rolling occurs at room temperature, resulting in finer surface finishes and tighter tolerances, often used for sheets and strips in precision applications. Both methods play crucial roles in industries such as automotive, construction, and aerospace, each serving distinct production needs and material properties.</p></p>
