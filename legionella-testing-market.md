<p><p>The Legionella testing market comprises several types, including microbial culture, DFA stain, PCR, and others. Microbial culture involves growing the bacteria in a lab to detect its presence, while DFA (Direct Fluorescent Antibody) stain uses fluorescent antibodies to identify Legionella in samples. PCR (Polymerase Chain Reaction) offers rapid detection by amplifying bacterial DNA. Other methods may include serological tests or antigen detection kits. Each method varies in sensitivity, speed, and application, catering to different diagnostic needs in public health and environmental monitoring.</p></p>
